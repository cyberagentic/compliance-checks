# DPA Quick Check — Requirements Catalog (9 Check Points)

## How to use this reference

Each check point maps directly to Art. 28 GDPR. The check uses a 3-level traffic light scale:

- **🟢 GREEN** — Requirement met
- **🟡 YELLOW** — Action needed — clause present but incomplete
- **🔴 RED** — Critical — clause missing or contradicts requirements

Third-country nexus (intake question 2) controls assessment depth for DPA-02 and DPA-05.

---

## DPA-01 — Written Form and Mandatory Contract Contents

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(9), Art. 28(3) sentence 1
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-01, DPA-02, DPA-03, DPA-04, DPA-05, DPA-06

### Review Criteria

1. Does the DPA exist in written or electronic form (Art. 28(9))?
2. Are both contracting parties clearly identified?
3. Are subject matter and purpose of processing concretely described?
4. Are the types of personal data listed (including Art. 9 special categories where applicable)?
5. Are the categories of data subjects named?
6. Is the duration of processing defined?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** DPA in proper form with all six mandatory content elements concretely specified.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** DPA exists but one or more content elements are too generic, incomplete, or unclear.
- **🔴 RED:** No written/electronic DPA, or multiple mandatory content elements entirely missing.

---

## DPA-02 — Documented Instructions

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(a), Art. 28(3) sentence 3
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-07, DPA-08
- **Third-country nexus flag:** If yes/unsure, verify that instruction binding explicitly covers third-country transfers.

### Review Criteria

1. Is processing limited to documented instructions from the controller?
2. Is the exception for legal obligations (Union/Member State law) regulated with prior notification?
3. Does the instruction binding explicitly cover transfers to third countries or international organizations?
4. Must the processor notify the controller immediately if an instruction is believed to violate GDPR or other data protection law?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Instruction binding comprehensive, including legal exception, third-country scope, and notification obligation for unlawful instructions.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Instruction binding present but incomplete (e.g., third-country scope missing, no notification obligation, no form requirement).
- **🔴 RED:** No instruction binding regulated.

---

## DPA-03 — Confidentiality

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(b)
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-09

### Review Criteria

1. Are persons authorized to process personal data committed to confidentiality or subject to statutory secrecy?
2. Does the obligation extend beyond the contract term?
3. Is processing outside the scope of instructions prohibited?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Confidentiality commitment or statutory obligation established, with continuation and instruction-scope limitation.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Confidentiality provision exists but incomplete (e.g., no continuation, no instruction-scope limitation).
- **🔴 RED:** No confidentiality obligation regulated.

---

## DPA-04 — Technical and Organizational Measures

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(c), Art. 32 GDPR
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-10

### Review Criteria

1. Is the processor obligated to implement all measures required under Art. 32 GDPR?
2. Are TOMs concretely described or documented in an annex (not merely referenced)?
3. Are the protection goals named (confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience)?
4. Has the risk to data subjects' rights and freedoms been considered?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** TOMs concretely documented with protection goals and risk consideration.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** TOMs mentioned but not concretely described, or no annex, or protection goals/risk assessment missing.
- **🔴 RED:** No provision for technical and organizational measures.

---

## DPA-05 — Sub-Processors

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(2), Art. 28(3)(d), Art. 28(4)
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-12, DPA-13
- **Third-country nexus flag:** If yes/unsure, verify that sub-processor provisions address third-country sub-processors and applicable safeguards.

### Review Criteria

1. Is prior specific or general written authorization from the controller required before engaging sub-processors?
2. If general authorization: Is there an information mechanism and a genuine right to object?
3. Are the same data protection obligations imposed on sub-processors by contract (Art. 28(4))?
4. Does the processor remain liable to the controller for the sub-processor's compliance?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Authorization requirement with information/objection mechanism, contractual flow-down, and processor liability clearly regulated.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Authorization exists but elements missing (objection right, flow-down incomplete, liability unclear).
- **🔴 RED:** No authorization requirement for sub-processors or no contractual flow-down.

---

## DPA-06 — Data Subject Rights Assistance

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(e)
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-15

### Review Criteria

1. Must the processor assist the controller in responding to data subject requests (Art. 15–22 GDPR)?
2. Is a forwarding procedure established for direct data subject contacts?
3. Is the nature of processing taken into account ("insofar as possible")?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Assistance obligation with forwarding procedure regulated, considering nature of processing.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Assistance obligation present but details missing (forwarding, procedure, timelines).
- **🔴 RED:** No assistance with data subject rights regulated.

---

## DPA-07 — Security and Notification Obligations

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(f), Art. 32–36 GDPR
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-16, DPA-17

### Review Criteria

1. Must the processor assist the controller with obligations under Art. 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation)?
2. Must the processor notify the controller without undue delay of any personal data breach?
3. Does the notification include minimum content (nature of breach, categories/numbers affected, likely consequences, measures taken)?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Assistance with Art. 32–36 comprehensive, breach notification with deadline and minimum content.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Assistance or notification present but incomplete (e.g., no deadline, missing content elements, not all Art. 32–36 areas covered).
- **🔴 RED:** No assistance with security obligations or no breach notification regulated.

---

## DPA-08 — Deletion and Return

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(g)
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-18

### Review Criteria

1. Must the processor delete or return all personal data after end of processing, at the controller's choice?
2. Must existing copies be deleted?
3. Is the legal retention exception regulated (Union/Member State law)?
4. Is a deadline or procedure for deletion/return specified?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Deletion/return with controller's right to choose, copy destruction, legal exception, and deadline/procedure.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Generally regulated but details missing (no right to choose, no deadline, no copy destruction).
- **🔴 RED:** No provision for deletion or return after end of processing.

---

## DPA-09 — Accountability and Audit

- **Legal Basis:** Art. 28(3)(h)
- **Mapped IDs:** DPA-20, DPA-21

### Review Criteria

1. Must the processor make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Art. 28 GDPR?
2. Must the processor allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections?
3. May audits be conducted by the controller or a mandated third-party auditor?
4. Are framework conditions for inspections regulated (advance notice, scope, confidentiality)?

### Assessment Criteria

- **🟢 GREEN:** Information disclosure, audit rights including inspections, third-party auditor option, and framework conditions regulated.
- **🟡 YELLOW:** Audit rights exist but restricted (no inspections, no third-party auditor, no framework).
- **🔴 RED:** No accountability obligations or no audit/inspection rights regulated.
