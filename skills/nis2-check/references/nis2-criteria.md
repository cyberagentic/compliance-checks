# NIS-2 Directive — Criteria for Applicability Assessment

Source: Directive (EU) 2022/2555 of 14 December 2022

---

## 1. SIZE THRESHOLDS (Article 2(1))

NIS-2 applies to public or private entities listed in Annex I or II that:
- Qualify as **medium-sized enterprises** or larger under EU Recommendation 2003/361/EC
- The SME definition thresholds are:

| Category        | Headcount | Annual Turnover | Annual Balance Sheet |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Micro           | < 10      | ≤ €2 million    | ≤ €2 million         |
| Small           | < 50      | ≤ €10 million   | ≤ €10 million        |
| Medium-sized    | < 250     | ≤ €50 million   | ≤ €43 million        |

**In-scope means**: The entity qualifies as at least medium-sized (≥ 50 employees OR turnover > €10M OR balance sheet > €10M) AND operates in a listed sector.

**Large entity**: ≥ 250 employees OR turnover > €50M OR balance sheet > €43M.

Note: For headcount + financial thresholds, an entity exceeds the "medium" ceiling if it has ≥ 250 staff OR turnover > €50M OR balance sheet > €43M. These large entities in Annex I sectors are automatically "essential."

Partner/linked enterprises: Member States may consider the degree of independence an entity has from partner/linked enterprises when calculating size (Article 6(2) of Recommendation 2003/361/EC Annex). Article 3(4) of the Annex to Recommendation 2003/361/EC does NOT apply.

---

## 2. SIZE-INDEPENDENT APPLICABILITY (Article 2(2)–(4))

Regardless of size, NIS-2 also applies when:

**(a) The entity is one of these specific types:**
- Providers of public electronic communications networks or publicly available electronic communications services
- Trust service providers (especially qualified trust service providers)
- Top-level domain (TLD) name registries
- DNS service providers

**(b)** The entity is the **sole provider** in a Member State of a service essential for critical societal or economic activities

**(c)** Disruption of the entity's service could have a **significant impact on public safety, public security, or public health**

**(d)** Disruption could induce a **significant systemic risk**, especially cross-border

**(e)** The entity is **critical because of specific importance** at national/regional level for its sector or interdependent sectors

**(f) Public administration entities:**
- Central government (as defined by Member State) — always in scope
- Regional level — in scope if risk-based assessment shows significant impact on critical societal/economic activities

**(Article 2(3))** Entities identified as **critical entities under CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557** — always in scope regardless of size

**(Article 2(4))** Entities providing **domain name registration services** — always in scope regardless of size

---

## 3. EXCLUSIONS (Article 2(6)–(10))

**Excluded:**
- Public administration entities carrying out activities **predominantly** in national security, public security, defence, or law enforcement (including prevention, investigation, detection, prosecution of criminal offences) — Article 2(7)
- Entities exempted from DORA (Regulation (EU) 2022/2554) scope per Article 2(4) of that regulation — Article 2(10)
- Member States' diplomatic and consular missions in third countries
- Entities jointly established with a third country under international agreement

**Conditional exemptions (Member State discretion):**
- Specific entities in national security/public security/defence/law enforcement may be exempted from obligations in Articles 21 or 23 — Article 2(8)
- Entities providing services exclusively to excluded public administration entities — Article 2(8)

**NOT excluded even if in security areas:**
- Trust service providers — Article 2(9) (always in scope if they provide trust services)

**Financial sector:**
- Financial entities covered by DORA (Regulation (EU) 2022/2554) follow DORA requirements instead of NIS-2 for risk management and incident reporting — but remain connected to NIS-2 framework for cooperation purposes

---

## 4. ESSENTIAL vs. IMPORTANT ENTITIES (Article 3)

### Essential entities (Article 3(1)):
- **(a)** Annex I entities that **exceed** medium-sized ceilings (i.e., large enterprises: ≥250 employees OR turnover >€50M OR balance sheet >€43M)
- **(b)** Qualified trust service providers, TLD name registries, DNS service providers — **regardless of size**
- **(c)** Providers of public electronic communications networks/services that qualify as **medium-sized** (≥50 and <250 employees, or turnover >€10M and ≤€50M)
- **(d)** Central government public administration entities
- **(e)** Any Annex I or II entity identified by Member State under Article 2(2)(b)–(e) special criteria
- **(f)** Critical entities under CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557
- **(g)** Entities previously identified as operators of essential services under old NIS Directive (if Member State decides so)

### Important entities (Article 3(2)):
- All other Annex I or II entities that are in scope but do NOT qualify as essential
- This includes medium-sized Annex I entities (unless they are telecom providers) and all in-scope Annex II entities

**Key distinction:** Essential entities face stricter supervision (ex ante + ex post), higher fines (up to €10M or 2% global turnover). Important entities face lighter supervision (ex post only), lower fines (up to €7M or 1.4% global turnover).

## 5. ANNEX I — SECTORS OF HIGH CRITICALITY

### 5.1 Energy
**Electricity:** Electricity supply undertakings, distribution system operators, transmission system operators, producers, nominated electricity market operators, market participants (aggregation/demand response/energy storage), EV recharging point operators
**District heating and cooling:** Operators of district heating or cooling
**Oil:** Oil transmission pipeline operators, oil production/refining/treatment/storage/transmission operators, central stockholding entities
**Gas:** Gas supply undertakings, distribution/transmission/storage/LNG system operators, natural gas undertakings, gas refining/treatment operators
**Hydrogen:** Hydrogen production, storage and transmission operators

### 5.2 Transport
**Air:** Commercial air carriers, airport managing bodies (including core TEN-T airports), air traffic control operators
**Rail:** Infrastructure managers, railway undertakings (including service facility operators)
**Water:** Inland/sea/coastal passenger & freight transport companies, port managing bodies (including port facilities), vessel traffic service (VTS) operators
**Road:** Road authorities responsible for traffic management control (excluding those where it's non-essential), Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS) operators

### 5.3 Banking
Credit institutions (as defined in CRR — Regulation EU 575/2013)

### 5.4 Financial Market Infrastructures
Operators of trading venues, Central counterparties (CCPs)

### 5.5 Health
Healthcare providers, EU reference laboratories, entities in R&D of medicinal products, manufacturers of basic pharmaceutical products and preparations (NACE C21), manufacturers of medical devices critical during public health emergencies

### 5.6 Drinking Water
Suppliers and distributors of water intended for human consumption (excluding those where this is non-essential part of activity)

### 5.7 Waste Water
Undertakings collecting, disposing of, or treating urban/domestic/industrial waste water (excluding those where this is non-essential)

### 5.8 Digital Infrastructure
Internet Exchange Point (IXP) providers, DNS service providers (excl. root name servers), TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, data centre service providers, content delivery network (CDN) providers, trust service providers, providers of public electronic communications networks, providers of publicly available electronic communications services

### 5.9 ICT Service Management (B2B)
Managed service providers (MSPs), Managed security service providers (MSSPs)

### 5.10 Public Administration
Central government entities (as defined by Member State), Regional public administration entities (as defined by Member State)

### 5.11 Space
Operators of ground-based infrastructure supporting space-based services (owned/managed/operated by Member States or private parties, excluding public electronic communications network providers)

---

## 6. ANNEX II — OTHER CRITICAL SECTORS

### 6.1 Postal and Courier Services
Postal service providers (as defined in Directive 97/67/EC), including courier services

### 6.2 Waste Management
Undertakings carrying out waste management (as defined in Directive 2008/98/EC), excluding those where waste management is not their principal economic activity

### 6.3 Manufacture, Production and Distribution of Chemicals
Undertakings manufacturing substances, distributing substances or mixtures, producing articles from substances or mixtures (under REACH Regulation)

### 6.4 Production, Processing and Distribution of Food
Food businesses engaged in **wholesale distribution** and **industrial production and processing** (not retail/restaurants)

### 6.5 Manufacturing
**(a)** Medical devices and in vitro diagnostic medical devices (except those already covered in Annex I point 5 — health sector critical devices)
**(b)** Computer, electronic and optical products (NACE C26)
**(c)** Electrical equipment (NACE C27)
**(d)** Machinery and equipment n.e.c. (NACE C28)
**(e)** Motor vehicles, trailers and semi-trailers (NACE C29)
**(f)** Other transport equipment (NACE C30)

### 6.6 Digital Providers
Providers of online marketplaces, online search engines, social networking services platforms

### 6.7 Research
Research organisations focused on applied research or experimental development with commercial exploitation (excluding educational institutions)

## 7. DECISION FLOWCHART

```
1. Is the entity in an Annex I or Annex II sector?
   NO → Not in scope (unless Member State special designation)
   YES → Continue

2. Is the entity size-independent per Article 2(2)-(4)?
   (telecom provider, trust service, TLD registry, DNS provider,
    domain registration service, sole provider, critical entity under CER,
    systemic importance, public administration central/regional)
   YES → In scope regardless of size → Go to step 4
   NO → Continue

3. Does the entity meet the medium-sized threshold?
   (≥ 50 employees OR turnover > €10M OR balance sheet > €10M)
   NO → Not in scope (unless Member State special designation)
   YES → In scope → Continue

4. Classification:
   - Annex I + large (≥250 emp OR turnover >€50M OR BS >€43M) → ESSENTIAL
   - Qualified trust service / TLD registry / DNS provider → ESSENTIAL (any size)
   - Public telecom network/service + medium-sized → ESSENTIAL
   - Central government public admin → ESSENTIAL
   - Critical entity under CER Directive → ESSENTIAL
   - Member State special designation under Art 2(2)(b)-(e) → ESSENTIAL
   - Annex I + medium-sized (not telecom) → IMPORTANT
   - Annex II + medium or large → IMPORTANT
   - Any other in-scope entity → IMPORTANT
```

## 8. KEY DEFINITIONS (Article 6, selected)

- **Managed service provider (MSP):** Entity providing services related to installation, management, operation or maintenance of ICT products, networks, infrastructure, applications, or other network and information systems, via assistance or active administration on customers' premises or remotely.
- **Managed security service provider (MSSP):** MSP that carries out or assists with cybersecurity risk management activities.
- **Cloud computing service:** Digital service enabling on-demand administration and broad remote access to a scalable and elastic pool of shareable computing resources (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, NaaS).
- **Data centre service:** Service encompassing structures dedicated to centralised accommodation, interconnection and operation of IT/network equipment providing data storage, processing and transport, with power distribution and environmental control. Does NOT include in-house corporate data centres.
- **Content delivery network (CDN):** Network of geographically distributed servers for high availability/fast delivery of digital content on behalf of content/service providers.
- **Research organisation:** Entity whose primary goal is applied research or experimental development with commercial exploitation. Excludes educational institutions.
- **Online marketplace / Online search engine / Social networking services platform:** As defined in respective EU regulations (Directive 2005/29/EC, Regulation 2019/1150, defined in Article 6).
